Article 2

STRUCTURE AND GOVERNANCE

This Article provides for the structure and governance of the Association by establishing the Executive Council as its principal administrative organ, defining its composition, tenure of office, powers, duties of officers, procedures for succession and vacancy, and the processes for removal or suspension of officers in accordance with this Constitution.

Section 1: Executive Council

The Association shall be governed by an Executive Council, which shall serve as the principal administrative and policy-making body of the Association.

The Executive Council shall consist of the following elected offices:

  1. President
  2. Vice President
  3. General Secretary
  4. Assistant General Secretary
  5. Financial Secretary
  6. Treasurer
  7. Publicity Secretary
  8. Assistant Publicity Secretary
  9. Social Director
  10. Welfare Coordinator
  11. Chief Whip

In addition to the elected offices, the Council shall include the following non-elected offices:

  1. Ex-officio

Section 2: Term of Office

  • Each officer of the Executive Council shall hold office for a term of two (2) years.
  • An officer may serve one (1) additional term only, subject to re-election or reappointment by the General Assembly.
  • This provision regarding tenure may be reviewed or amended as deemed necessary by the General Assembly.

Section 3: Authority and Functions

The Executive Council shall:

  • Provide leadership and strategic direction for the Association.
  • Oversee the implementation of all policies, programmes, and decisions of the General Assembly.
  • Manage the administrative, financial, and operational affairs of the Association.
  • Represent the Association in all official matters, both internally and externally.

Section 4: Section 4: Roles and Duties of Executive Council Officers

(a) President:

The President shall be the chief executive officer and overall head of the Association.

Duties:

  • Provide visionary leadership and direction for the Association.
  • Preside over all meetings of the Executive Council and General Assembly.
  • Represent the Association in official matters, events, and communications.
  • Oversee the activities of all teams and ensure effective coordination and collaboration.
  • Endorse all official correspondence and financial transactions approved by the Council.

(b) Vice President:

The Vice President shall serve as the principal assistant to the President and shall act on behalf of the President in the President's absence.

Duties:

  • Assist the President in the performance of all duties.
  • Act on behalf of the President in their absence.
  • Coordinate special projects and committees as directed by the President or the Executive Council.
  • Provide support in monitoring the performance and compliance of officers.

(c) General Secretary:

The General Secretary shall serve as the principal record keeper and administrative liaison of the Association.

Duties:

  • Prepare and circulate meeting notices, agendas, and minutes.
  • Maintain an up-to-date register of members and handle all official correspondence.
  • Serve as custodian of the Association's official documents and seal.
  • Communicate resolutions and directives of the Executive Council to members.

(d) Assistant General Secretary:

The Assistant General Secretary shall support the General Secretary in administrative duties and act in their stead when necessary.

Duties:

  • Assist the General Secretary in all administrative and secretarial duties.
  • Act on behalf of the General Secretary in their absence.
  • Support the preparation and circulation of meeting notices, agendas, and minutes.
  • Help maintain accurate records, documents, and correspondence of the Association.
  • Assist in managing the membership register and internal communication.
  • Coordinate with other officers to ensure the timely submission of reports and updates.
  • Perform any additional duties assigned by the General Secretary, President, or the Executive Council.

(e) Financial Secretary:

The Financial Secretary shall serve as the officer responsible for managing all revenues, record-keeping of income, and financial documentation.

Duties:

  • Collect and record all dues, levies, donations, and other revenues.
  • Maintain accurate financial records and issue receipts for collections.
  • Work closely with the Treasurer to reconcile accounts and prepare financial statements.
  • Facilitate fundraising activities and financial planning.
  • Provide monthly financial updates to the Executive Council.

(f) Treasurer:

The Treasurer shall serve as the chief custodian of all financial assets and accounts of the Association.

Duties:

  • Serve as the chief custodian of all Association funds.
  • Oversee all income, expenditures, and financial transactions.
  • Ensure proper documentation, accountability, and adherence to financial policies.
  • Operate the Association's accounts jointly with other designated signatories.

(g) Chief Whip:

The Chief Whip shall ensure discipline, order, and adherence to the Association's rules and decisions.

Duties:

  • Maintain decorum during meetings and official gatherings.
  • Ensure that members comply with the provisions of the Constitution and decisions of the Council.
  • Mediate minor disputes among members and promote harmony within the Association.
  • Support the enforcement of disciplinary measures as approved by the Executive Council.

(h) Publicity Secretary:

The Publicity Secretary shall serve as the principal communications and media relations officer of the Association.

Duties:

  • Manage the public image and communication channels of the Association.
  • Disseminate official information, announcements, and updates.
  • Develop publicity materials and oversee media relations.
  • Promote events, achievements, and initiatives of the Association.
  • Manage digital platforms, including social media and online communication.

(i) Assistant Publicity Secretary:

The Assistant Publicity Secretary shall serve as the supporting communications officer of the Association, assisting the Publicity Secretary.

Duties:

  • Act on behalf of the Publicity Secretary in their absence to ensure continuity of communication and public engagement.
  • Assist the Publicity Secretary in preparing and disseminating official announcements, updates, and publicity materials.
  • Support the management of the Association's communication channels, including social media and digital platforms.
  • Help coordinate publicity for events, programmes, and activities of the Association.
  • Maintain records of publicity materials and media content.
  • Perform any additional communication-related duties assigned by the Publicity Secretary or the Executive Council.

(j) Social Director:

The Social Director shall oversee the planning, coordination, and execution of social, recreational, and community-building activities that promote unity, engagement, and positive interaction among members.

Duties:

  • Plan and coordinate social, cultural, and recreational programmes.
  • Promote member engagement through events and community activities.
  • Collaborate with the PRO to publicise events.
  • Recommend partnerships to enhance social impact.

(k) Welfare Coordinator:

The Welfare Coordinator shall manage the welfare affairs of the Association, ensuring the well-being, support, and compassionate response to the needs of members, while recommending programmes that enhance member welfare.

Duties:

  • Oversee all matters relating to the well-being, support, and interests of members.
  • Respond to welfare needs, emergencies, and special cases brought to the Association.
  • Propose and implement welfare initiatives, interventions, and support schemes.
  • Maintain confidentiality when handling sensitive welfare matters.

(l) Ex Officio:

The Ex Officio shall serve on the Executive Council by virtue of holding or having held a specific office, providing advisory support, guidance, and institutional memory to ensure continuity and effective functioning of the Council.

The following individuals shall automatically become Ex-Officio Members upon completion of their tenure: The Immediate Past President and the Immediate Past General Secretary.

Duties:

  • Serve in an advisory capacity to provide continuity from the previous administration.
  • Offer guidance, institutional memory, and strategic insight when required.
  • Attend Executive Council meetings in a non-voting capacity.
  • Perform or support tasks as may be assigned by the President or Executive Council.

If either or both individuals decline the right to serve as Ex-Officio Members, the incumbent Executive Council shall reserve the authority to appoint any two past Executive Officers deemed fit to serve in this capacity. Such appointments shall be based on merit, experience, and the value the appointees can offer in providing continuity and advisory support to the Council.

Section 5: Succession and Vacancy

5.1: Succession

The Alumni Association shall maintain a clear and orderly succession process to ensure continuity of leadership and effective governance. If an officer is unable to perform their duties due to resignation, suspension, incapacity, removal, or death, succession shall follow the provisions outlined in this Constitution:

  • Where applicable, the Deputy or Assistant to any substantive officer shall immediately assume acting responsibilities until the General Assembly or Executive Council takes a formal decision.
  • In the absence of a designated deputy or assistant, the Executive Council shall appoint the most senior or suitably qualified Council member to serve in an acting capacity.
  • Acting officers shall exercise powers only to maintain operations and may not introduce major reforms without approval from the Executive Council or General Assembly.

A formal handover process shall be conducted whenever leadership changes occur. This includes:

  • Transfer of all documents, materials, and assets.
  • Submission of handover notes.
  • A brief report of unfinished tasks and ongoing commitments.

5.2: Vacancy in Office

A vacancy shall be deemed to occur in any office when:

  • An officer resigns in writing.
  • An officer is suspended or removed from office.
  • An officer becomes medically or physically incapacitated.
  • An officer is absent from duties without reasonable cause for a period defined by the By-laws.
  • An officer is convicted of misconduct, fraud, or any acts deemed detrimental to the Association.
  • An officer dies.

Upon confirmation of a vacancy, the General Secretary shall issue a formal notice to the Executive Council and General Assembly within a reasonable timeframe.

Vacant offices shall be filled as follows:

  • If the remaining tenure is less than half of the officer's term, the Executive Council may appoint an eligible member to complete the term.
  • If the remaining tenure is more than half, a by-election shall be conducted by the Electoral Committee within a period defined by the Constitution.
  • Appointed officers filling a vacancy shall possess all the full rights and responsibilities of the office for the remainder of the term.

Succession to any vacant office shall occur within one (1) month in the case of a non-death vacancy, and within two (2) months where the vacancy arises as a result of the death of an office holder.

5.3: Temporary Absences

The following provisions apply to temporary absences of officers:

  • Officers who require a temporary leave of absence must formally notify the Executive Council.
  • During an approved temporary absence, the Deputy or Assistant Officer shall assume acting responsibilities.
  • Temporary absence exceeding a period defined by the By-laws may trigger a review to determine if the position should be declared vacant.

5.4: Continuity of Operations

The following measures shall ensure continuity of operations during leadership transitions:

  • The Executive Council shall ensure that core administrative, financial, and program-related functions of the Alumni Association continue uninterrupted during transitions.
  • No leadership transition shall hinder ongoing alumni programs, partnerships, or institutional relationships.

Section 6: Removal or Suspension from Office

6.1: Grounds for Removal or Suspension

An officer of the Executive Council may be removed or suspended from office on any of the following grounds:

  • Misconduct, including acts that contravene the values, objectives, or constitution of the Association.
  • Neglect of duties, failure to perform assigned responsibilities, or persistent non-attendance at meetings.
  • Misappropriation, embezzlement, or misuse of the Associationâ€™s funds or property.
  • Any action deemed detrimental to the interests or reputation of the Association.

6.2: Initiation of Removal or Suspension

A motion for removal or suspension may be initiated by:

  • At least one-third of the Executive Council members, or
  • At least one-third of the General Assembly members.

The motion must be submitted in writing to the General Secretary, stating the specific grounds and supporting evidence.

6.3: Procedure

Upon receipt of a removal or suspension motion, the General Secretary shall convene an Emergency Executive Council Meeting or a General Assembly Meeting to review the matter.

  • The officer in question shall be given adequate notice and the opportunity to present a defence or explanation.
  • A fair and impartial hearing shall be conducted, with all relevant evidence presented.

The motion must be submitted in writing to the General Secretary, stating the specific grounds and supporting evidence.

6.4: Voting and Decision

  • A decision to remove or suspend an officer shall require a two-thirds majority vote of the members present at the meeting (Executive Council or General Assembly, as applicable).
  • In the case of suspension, the duration and conditions of suspension shall be clearly defined.
  • The decision of the General Assembly shall be final and binding, except where an appeal is provided for in this constitution.

6.5: Interim Arrangements

  • If an officer is suspended or removed, the Executive Council may appoint an acting officer to temporarily fulfil the duties of the office until a formal election or ratification is conducted.
  • The acting officer shall serve under the same responsibilities as the original officeholder, without prejudice to the final decision of the General Assembly.

6.6: Appeal

  • An officer who has been removed or suspended may appeal the decision in writing to the General Assembly within a specified period (e.g., 30 days).
  • The General Assembly shall review the appeal and render a final decision, which shall be binding on all parties.
