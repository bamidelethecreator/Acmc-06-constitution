Article 3

MEMBERSHIP

This Article establishes the categories, eligibility, rights, obligations, and disciplinary provisions relating to membership of the Association in accordance with this Constitution.

Section 1: Eligibility

Membership in the Association shall be open to individuals who meet the following criteria:

  • Any person who was officially registered as a student of African Church Model College for any period whatsoever during the academic sessions commencing 2000/2001 and ending 2005/2006.
  • Must demonstrate good character and commitment to the objectives of the Alumni Association.
  • Must satisfy any additional requirements as may be determined by the General Assembly.

Section 2: Rights and Responsibilities

2.1: Rights

Members shall have the right to:

  • Attend and participate in General Assembly meetings.
  • Vote in elections and on matters affecting the Association.
  • Stand for election to any office within the Association.
  • Receive information about the Association's activities, programmes, and finances.

2.2: Responsibilities

Members responsibility shall be to:

  • Uphold the values, objectives, and rules of the Association.
  • Pay any membership dues or levies as determined by the General Assembly
  • Participate actively in alumni programmes, events, and initiatives.

Section 3: Cessation of Membership

3.1: Voluntary Cessation

  • A member may voluntarily cease membership by submitting a written notice to the General Secretary. Such cessation shall take effect upon receipt of the notice by the General Secretary.
  • Voluntary cessation of membership shall not entitle the member to any refund of dues or levies paid.
  • A member who voluntarily ceases membership may reapply for membership in the future, subject to the eligibility criteria and approval by the General Assembly.
  • Voluntary cessation of membership shall not affect the member's obligations or liabilities incurred prior to cessation, including any outstanding dues or levies.

3.2: Disciplinary Action

  • A member may be subject to disciplinary action, including suspension or expulsion, for conduct that is detrimental to the Association or violates its rules and regulations.
  • Disciplinary action shall be initiated by the Executive Council and decided upon by the General Assembly in accordance with due process and the principles of natural justice.
  • A member facing disciplinary action shall have the right to be heard and to present a defence before any decision is made.
  • The decision of the General Assembly regarding disciplinary action shall be final and binding on all parties involved.

3.3: Conduct Harmful to the Association

  • Any member whose conduct is deemed harmful to the reputation, interests, or objectives of the Association may be subject to disciplinary action as outlined in Section 3.2.
  • Conduct that may be considered harmful includes, but is not limited to, actions that bring the Association into disrepute, violate its code of conduct, or undermine its unity and cohesion.
  • The Executive Council shall have the authority to investigate any allegations of harmful conduct and recommend appropriate disciplinary measures to the General Assembly.

3.4: Death

Membership automatically ends upon the passing of a member.
