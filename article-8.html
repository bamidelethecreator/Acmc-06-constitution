Article 8

CODE OF CONDUCT & ETHICS

This Article establishes the requirements for membership dues within the Association, outlining the obligation of members to make financial contributions, the structure for payment, and the role these dues play in supporting the activities and operations of the Association.

Section 1: General Code of Conduct

Members and officers of the Association shall:

  1. Act with honesty, integrity, fairness, and respect in all Association matters.
  2. Uphold the Constitution, By-laws, and lawful decisions of the General Assembly and Executive Council.
  3. Avoid any conduct that may bring the Association into disrepute or expose it to legal, financial, or reputational risk.
  4. Maintain professionalism in physical meetings, online platforms, and all official communications.
  5. Refrain from discrimination, harassment, abusive language, or conduct capable of causing division or hostility among members.
  6. Declare any conflict of interest in matters where personal, financial, or professional interests may influence decisions.

Section 2: Misconduct

Misconduct shall include, but not be limited to:

  1. Act with honesty, integrity, fairness, and respect in all Association matters.
  2. Violation of any provision of this Constitution or By-laws.
  3. Gross negligence, abuse of office, or failure to perform assigned duties.
  4. Financial impropriety, fraud, embezzlement, or misrepresentation.
  5. Conduct that undermines unity, peace, or the objectives of the Association.
  6. Public or private actions that damage the reputation of the Association.

Section 3: Disciplinary Measures

Depending on the severity of the offence, disciplinary measures may include:

  1. Verbal or written warning.
  2. Suspension of membership rights or office.
  3. Removal from office.
  4. Expulsion from the Association.

All disciplinary actions shall be proportionate, documented, and communicated in writing to the affected member or officer.
