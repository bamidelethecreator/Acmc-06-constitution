Article 9

RELATIONSHIP WITH THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY

This Article defines the relationship with the General Assembly, outlining the structure of interaction, lines of authority and accountability, and the role the General Assembly plays in guiding, overseeing, and supporting the activities and decisions of the Association.

Section 1: Supreme Authority

The General Assembly, comprising all registered financial members of the Association, shall be the supreme decision-making authority of the Association.
All powers not expressly delegated by this Constitution to any other organ of the Association shall reside in the General Assembly.

Section 2: Powers of the General Assembly

The General Assembly shall have the power to:

  1. Adopt, amend, and interpret this Constitution and any By-laws made pursuant to it.
  2. Elect and remove members of the Executive Council and appoint Trustees where applicable.
  3. Approve annual budgets, financial statements, membership dues, and major policie
  4. Ratify major decisions, projects, and programmes of the Association.
  5. Approve the appointment of auditors and receive audit reports.
  6. Dissolve the Association in accordance with the provisions of this Constitution.

Section 3: Limitations of Authority

The Executive Council shall not:

  1. Alter or amend this Constitution without the approval of the General Assembly.
  2. Commit the Association to major financial obligations without prior approval.
  3. Take actions inconsistent with the resolutions or directives of the General Assembly.

All disciplinary actions shall be proportionate, documented, and communicated in writing to the affected member or officer.

Section 4: Conflict Resolution Between Organs

In the event of any conflict between the decisions of the Executive Council and the General Assembly, the decision of the General Assembly shall prevail.
