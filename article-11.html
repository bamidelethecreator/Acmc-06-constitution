Article 11

DISSOLUTION

This Article establishes the procedures for dissolution, outlining the conditions under which the Association may be formally dissolved, the process for winding up its affairs, and the role these procedures play in ensuring an orderly and responsible closure.

Section 1: Authority to Dissolve

The Association may be dissolved only by a resolution passed at a General Assembly meeting, where at least two-thirds (⅔) of all registered members are present and voting.

Section 2: Proposal of Dissolution

A proposal for dissolution may be made by:

  1. The Executive Council, or
  2. At least one-third (⅓) of the registered members.

Written notice of the proposed dissolution must be circulated to all members at least 30 days prior to the meeting.

Section 3: Voting Requirement

A two-thirds (⅔) majority of members present and voting is required to approve the dissolution.
Voting shall be by secret ballot unless otherwise determined by the General Assembly.

Section 4: Distribution of Assets

Upon dissolution, after payment of all debts and liabilities, the remaining assets shall not be distributed among members or Trustees.
Instead, the assets shall be transferred to another registered non-profit, educational, or charitable organization in Nigeria with similar objectives, as approved by the General Assembly.
