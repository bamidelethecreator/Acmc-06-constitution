Article 6

COMMITTEES

This Article establishes the formation and functions of committees within the Association, outlining their roles in supporting the leadership, carrying out specific tasks or projects, and assisting in the effective implementation of the Association's activities and objectives.

Section 1: Establishment of Committees

The Executive Council shall have the power to establish standing and ad-hoc committees as it deems necessary for the effective administration and achievement of the objectives of the Association. The Association shall operate the following committees:

Section 2: Standing Committees (Ongoing)

The following shall be recognised as Standing Committees of the Association:

6.2.1: Project/Development Committee

Shall be responsible for planning, coordinating, and supervising projects and development initiatives of the Association, subject to the approval and direction of the Executive Council.

6.2.2:Welfare Committee

Shall be responsible for matters relating to the welfare and well-being of members of the Association and shall operate under the supervision of the Executive Council.

6.2.3:Electoral Committee

Shall be responsible for organising, conducting, and supervising elections of the Association in accordance with the provisions of this Constitution and shall submit its reports to the Executive Council.

6.2.4:Audit Committee

Shall be responsible for examining the financial records of the Association and ensuring accountability, and shall report its findings to the Executive Council and the General Assembly, where applicable.

Section 3: Ad-Hoc Committees (Temporary)

The Executive Council shall have the power to constitute ad-hoc committees to carry out specific assignments or time-bound tasks. Such committees shall be dissolved upon the completion of their assigned duties.

Section 4:Appointment and Composition

The Executive Council shall appoint the Chairperson and members of all committees and shall determine their composition based on suitability and relevance to assigned duties.

Section 5: Tenure of Committees

The Executive Council shall determine the tenure of all committees and shall not extend beyond the tenure of the Executive Council that constituted them, except where otherwise approved by the General Assembly.

Section 6: Reporting and Accountability

All committees shall report to the Executive Council and shall be accountable to it. Committees may make recommendations, but shall not take binding decisions or commit the Association without the prior approval of the Executive Council.

Section 7: Removal and Replacement

The Executive Council shall have the power to remove or replace any committee member for misconduct, inability to perform assigned duties, or prolonged inactivity. Any vacancy arising shall be filled by the Executive Council.
