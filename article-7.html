Article 7

MEMBERSHIP DUES

This Article establishes the requirements for membership dues within the Association, outlining the obligation of members to make financial contributions, the structure for payment, and the role these dues play in supporting the activities and operations of the Association.

Section 1: Purpose of Dues

Membership dues shall serve as the primary source of funding for the operations, programs, and projects of the Association. They shall ensure the smooth running of activities, support for members' welfare, and the sustainability of the Association's initiatives.

Section 2: Annual and/or Monthly Dues

The Association shall levy annual dues on all members, the amount of which shall be determined by the Executive Council and approved by the General Assembly. The Executive Council may, at its discretion, allow for monthly or quarterly payment options to facilitate ease of contribution. All members are expected to pay dues in a timely manner to remain in good standing.

Section 3: Determination of Amount

The Executive Council shall determine the amount of dues based on the operational requirements of the Association, prevailing economic conditions, and members' capacity to contribute. Any change to the dues shall be communicated to all members in advance and approved by the General Assembly.

Section 4: Review of Dues

Membership dues shall be reviewed annually by the Executive Council. The review shall consider economic changes, inflation, and the financial needs of the Association. Any adjustment in dues shall be presented for approval at the General Assembly.

Section 5: Collection and Enforcement

The Executive Council shall determine the method and schedule for collecting dues. Members who fail to pay dues within the stipulated time may forfeit certain rights and privileges, including voting rights and eligibility for office, until payment is made. The Executive Council shall ensure transparency and accountability in the management of dues collected.
