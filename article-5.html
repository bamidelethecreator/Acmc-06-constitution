Article 5

MEETINGS AND QUORUM

This Article establishes the procedures for holding meetings within the Association and defines the quorum required for meetings to be valid. It outlines how meetings are convened, the requirement for proper notification to members, and the minimum number of members that must be present before decisions can be made, ensuring that the Association's activities and resolutions are conducted in an orderly and legitimate manner.

Section 1: Types and Frequency of Meetings

a. Executive Council Meetings

  • The Executive Council shall meet at least once every month to review activities, plan programs, and address administrative matters.
  • Emergency Executive Meetings may be convened at the request of the President or upon written request by at least one-third of Executive Council members.

b. General Meetings

  • The Alumni Association shall hold a General Meeting once every quarter, except where otherwise approved by the Executive Council.
  • An Annual General Meeting (AGM) shall be held once every year to review the Association's progress, present financial reports, and conduct elections where applicable.
  • Extraordinary General Meetings may be convened as required, especially in matters of urgency or major interest to the general membership.

Section 2: Notice of Meetings

a. Executive Council Meetings:
A minimum notice of 48 hours shall be given for regular Executive Meetings, and not less than 24 hours for emergency meetings.
b. General Meetings:
A minimum notice of seven (14) days shall be given for regular General Meetings, while AGMs shall require at least twenty-one (21) days' notice. Notices may be issued via email, text message, official social platforms, or any other approved communication channel.

Section 3: Quorum Requirements

a. Executive Council Meetings:
A quorum shall consist of one-half (½) of the Executive Council members, including at least one of the following officers: President Vice President General Secretary.
b. General Meetings:
A quorum for a General Meeting shall be one-third (⅓) of the registered financial members of the Association. Where a quorum is not met within thirty (30) minutes of the scheduled start time, the meeting may be adjourned or reconvened at a later date as determined by the presiding officer.

Section 4: Voting Procedures

a. Eligibility:
Only financial members in good standing are eligible to vote during General Meetings, while all Executive Officers present may vote during Executive Meetings.
b. Method of Voting:
Voting may be conducted through any of the following methods, as determined by the presiding officer:

  • Voice vote
  • Show of hands
  • Ballot (open or secret)
  • Electronic voting, where applicable
c. Decision Making:

  • Except otherwise stated in this Constitution, all decisions shall be made by a simple majority of votes cast.
  • In the event of a tie, the Presiding Officer shall have a casting vote.
  • Constitutional amendments or major policy changes may require a two-thirds (⅔) majority of members present and voting.
