Article 4

ACCOUNTABILITY AND REPORTING

This Article establishes the Association's identity, legal status, objectives, guiding principles, and approved communication channels in accordance with applicable laws.

Section 1: Transparency in Leadership Activities

  • All elected and appointed officers of the Alumni Association shall carry out their duties openly and transparently.
  • Officers must provide accurate and timely updates regarding their activities, decisions, and use of Association resources.
  • Transparency shall be upheld as a core principle to foster trust, unity, and responsible alumni governance.

Section 2: Regular Reporting Obligations

Executive Council Reports

  • The Executive Council shall prepare and present periodic reports quarterly or as determined by the General Assembly.
  • These reports shall cover alumni engagement activities, programs, finances, partnerships, challenges, and recommendations.

Committee Reports

  • All Standing and Ad-hoc Committees shall submit written reports of their activities before every General Assembly meeting.
  • Reports shall outline achievements, expenditures, challenges, and proposals for future alumni programs.

Annual Report
At the Annual General Meeting (AGM), the Association shall receive a comprehensive Annual Report comprising:

  • Administrative and leadership activities.
  • Alumni programs, events, and impact reports.
  • Financial statements and auditor's observations (where applicable).
  • Membership growth, networking initiatives, and alumni outreach.
  • Strategic goals and recommendations for the upcoming year.

Section 3: Financial Accountability

  • All financial transactions of the Alumni Association shall follow approved procedures and established accounting standards.
  • The Financial Secretary and Treasurer shall jointly maintain accurate financial records and prepare quarterly financial statements.
  • The Association's accounts shall undergo an annual internal or external audit, depending on the scale and resources of the Association.

  • Draft budgets, financial reports, and expenditure records must be submitted to the General Assembly for review and approval.

Section 4: Monitoring and Evaluation (M&E)

  • The Executive Council shall implement mechanisms to monitor ongoing alumni projects, outreach initiatives, and development programs.
  • Completed programs shall be evaluated to assess impact, effectiveness, and sustainability.
  • Findings and lessons learned shall guide planning and improvement of future alumni activities.

Section 5: Member Oversight and Access to Information

  • Members of the Alumni Association have the right to request clarification or access to non-confidential records such as financial reports, decisions, program updates, and minutes.
  • Such requests shall be addressed promptly and in accordance with uidelines established by the Executive Council.
  • Members may call for accountability on matters affecting alumni interests, finances, or decisions inconsistent with the Constitution.

Section 6: Accountability in Conduct

  • All officers and members acting on behalf of the Alumni Association shall uphold the Association's values, ethics, and code of conduct.
  • Misconduct, misrepresentation, misuse of authority, or mismanagement of funds shall attract disciplinary actions as outlined in the Constitution and By-laws.
  • The Executive Council shall investigate reported violations and recommend appropriate sanctions.

Section 7: Reporting Channels and Documentation

  • All official reporting shall be communicated through approved channels such as the Secretariat, official email platforms, or designated alumni communication portals.
  • The Secretary-General shall ensure that minutes, resolutions, reports, and official documents are properly recorded, archived, and secured.
  • Archival documentation shall be preserved for institutional continuity, accountability, and reference purposes.

Section 8: Feedback and Continuous Improvements

  • The Alumni Association shall maintain an accessible feedback system where members can submit suggestions, observations, commendations, or concerns.
  • The Executive Council shall periodically review feedback and integrate relevant insights into program planning, governance, and alumni engagement strategies.
  • Continuous improvement shall guide all alumni initiatives to enhance participation, representation, and overall impact.
