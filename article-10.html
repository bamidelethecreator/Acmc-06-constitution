Article 10

ADOPTION AND AMENDMENT

This Article establishes the procedures for the adoption and amendment of this constitution, outlining the process through which the Association's provisions are approved, reviewed, and modified, and the role these procedures play in ensuring adaptability and continued relevance.

Section 1: Adoption

This constitution shall become operative directly after it is approved by the General Assembly at an Annual General Meeting or an extraordinary meeting scheduled for that purpose.

Section 2: Power to Amend

The Constitution of the ACMC 2006 Alumni Association may be amended, altered, or repealed only by the General Assembly, which is the supreme authority of the Association.

Section 3: Proposal of Amendments

  1. At least one-third (⅓) of the registered financial members, or The Executive Council of the Association
  2. Proposed amendments must be submitted to the General Secretary at least thirty (30) days prior to the meeting where they will be considered.

Section 4: Notice of Amendment

  1. The General Secretary shall circulate the proposed amendments to all registered members at least fourteen (14) days prior to the meeting.
  2. The notice shall include the full text of the proposed amendment(s) and an explanation of the intended changes.

Section 5: Consideration and Voting

  1. Amendments shall be considered at a duly convened General Assembly meeting.
  2. A two-thirds (⅔) majority of members present and voting is required to approve any amendment.
  3. Voting shall be conducted by secret ballot unless otherwise determined by the General Assembly.

Section 6: Compliance

All amendments must comply with the laws of the Federal Republic of Nigeria and shall take effect immediately upon approval by the General Assembly, unless otherwise specified in the resolution.

Section 7: Documentation

The General Secretary shall ensure that all amendments are recorded, filed, and reflected in the official copy of the Constitution. Updated copies shall be distributed to all members.
